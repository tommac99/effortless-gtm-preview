i. Position

The thing that actually changed in B2B sales.

For a decade, outbound was a function of volume. The team that pushed more emails, made more calls, scraped more lists, won. Then deliverability collapsed, attention collapsed, and the buyers who used to forgive a half-relevant message stopped replying entirely. Volume stopped working roughly the same week LinkedIn auto-suggested its first dreadful icebreaker.

What replaced volume is signal. A new VP joins a target account. A funding round closes. A competitor exits a category. A job posting hints at a new initiative. Each is a narrow, time-boxed window of attention — and a buyer who is more likely to reply this week than they will be in three months.

Most B2B teams know this intellectually. Almost none of them have a system that does it. We build that system. We do it with AI where AI helps, with people where people are required, and with EU-resident infrastructure because half our market cares about that.

If you cannot put a number on your pipeline gap, you do not have a sales problem. You have a measurement problem — and that one we can fix in an afternoon.

Three problems. One audit tells you which one is yours.

i.Problem
A lead-quantity problem.
You ship a great product, your win-rate is healthy, you simply do not see enough deals. The reps work hard but the top of the funnel is malnourished. The fix is upstream: signal sources, fresh lists, a way to know which 300 companies are ready to hear from you this quarter.
ii.Problem
A conversion problem.
Leads land, leads disappear. Reply rates are below four percent. Meetings get booked, then ghosted. Reps blame the list, marketing blames the reps, nobody blames the timing. The fix is the timing — outreach fired the day the signal lands, not three weeks later.
iii.Problem
A time problem.
Plenty of leads, decent conversion, but everyone is burnt out and the reps sell for two hours a day. The other six are spreadsheets. The fix is workflow — quietly removing the steps that should never have been a human's job in the first place.
ii. Offer

How it begins. Always the same way.

It begins with the 5-minute audit. Ten questions about your motion, your numbers, your stack. Then a PDF — generated, not template-filled — that shows your sales-org maturity, your pipeline gap in euros, and one quick-win you can run on Monday.

If we are a fit, you choose. A Quick Win (one engagement, two to five days, €1,500–€3,000) to prove value before you commit. Or the Signal System (five to fifteen thousand to build, retainer to run) for teams who want the whole machine. There is also a third option, but it requires a conversation.

The audit is yours either way. So is the PDF. Bring it to your CFO. Use it to negotiate with a different vendor. Use it as a scoreboard for your reps. We don't mind. The audit is the start of the relationship, not a sales pitch dressed as a lead magnet.

iii. Method

Built on Bedrock, run from the EU.

We use Claude via AWS Bedrock, deployed in EU regions. That matters if your data, your customers, or your legal counsel cares about where prompts are processed. It also matters because the alternative — gluing six American SaaS tools together with a Zapier prayer — is exactly the kind of mess we were built to clean up.

Everything we build is yours. Sources, prompts, scoring, sequences, dashboards. If you ever stop working with us, you do not lose your system. That is the deal.
